Non-GAAP Definition

EBITDA, a non-GAAP financial measure, refers to earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization. We provided our EBITDA, as well as 2020 EBITDA for a peer group of technology and general industry companies (calculated using the most comparable line items from their publicly reported financial statements), for fiscal year 2020 to provide investors with additional insight into how our Compensation Committee determined our peer group for assessing pay practices. Management does not utilize EBITDA in evaluating business performance. EBITDA should not be considered as a substitute for, or superior to, net income prepared in accordance with GAAP as a measure of financial performance.
